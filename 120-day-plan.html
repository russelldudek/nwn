Candidate vision
Operating principle

Build credibility before bureaucracy.

Learn the real transaction, make priorities explicit, prove one useful mechanism, and institutionalize only what the evidence supports. The plan assumes executive sponsorship, access to process owners, and a bounded pilot opportunity; all specifics require internal discovery.

Guardrails across all 120 days

No invented certainty

Separate verified fact, current hypothesis, assumption, and open question in every decision artifact.

No parallel bureaucracy

OPE owns the mechanism and challenge process; business leaders retain outcome and operating ownership.

No metric without action

Every KPI needs a definition, owner, review cadence, threshold, and expected decision.

No launch without adoption

Configuration, SOPs, training, support, controls, and escalation are part of readiness—not post-launch cleanup.

Day-120 executive readout

What we learned about the O2C system · What one pilot changed · What value and risk evidence says · What remains unresolved · What the readiness gate should govern · What the next portfolio should prioritize · What sponsorship and investment the function needs
