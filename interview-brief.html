Candidate vision
Mandate hypothesis

Build an O2C operating system that makes growth transactable.

Establish Operations Process Excellence from zero; make O2C performance visible; govern the process-optimization portfolio; and create a readiness gate that reconciles commercial promise with systems, finance, fulfillment, billing, support, and adoption before launch.

Why now

NWN publicly positions itself as an AI-powered technology-solutions and managed-services provider serving thousands of complex organizations. A broadening portfolio, recurring services, bundles, pricing models, and system dependencies increase the cost of discovering transaction design after launch.

White-space thesis

OPE should not become a centralized cleanup desk. It should operate as a portfolio-and-readiness function: choose the highest-value process work, clarify end-to-end ownership, govern change, instrument performance, and return sustainable capability to the business.

Success contract

  • Faster O2C flow without control degradation.
  • Fewer order and invoice defects.
  • Lower unbilled inventory and margin leakage.
  • Explicit ownership of exceptions and process change.
  • New offerings transact cleanly at launch.
  • Teams can execute without recurring heroics.

Decision rights to clarify

  • Who owns the end-to-end O2C outcome?
  • Who can approve, time-box, or retire exceptions?
  • Who defines release evidence for new offerings?
  • Who resolves conflicts across Sales, Finance, Operations, PMO, and IT?
  • Who owns sustained adoption after implementation?

Five operating tensions

Speed vs readiness

Accelerate launches without creating transaction debt.

Standard vs complexity

Scale one operating language while bounding legitimate differences.

Integrity vs exception

Serve urgent needs without institutionalizing workarounds.

Visibility vs action

Make metrics trigger decisions rather than reporting rituals.

Center vs ownership

Build OPE strength without becoming the permanent process owner.

Automation vs clarity

Simplify the work and authority model before automating it.

Strongest objection

Concern: no prior formal enterprise O2C transformation title or claimed ownership of NWN’s exact NetSuite, ServiceNow, Salesforce, and revenue-recognition architecture.

Response: acknowledge the gap. Bring verified adjacent operating depth across ERP/CRM workflows, commercial-to-fulfillment handoffs, production, quality, customer delivery, process controls, training, escalation, and performance visibility. Learn the domain from incumbent experts before prescribing.

Evidence that transfers

  • Vape-Jet: cross-functional AI-first operations, ERP/CRM/workflow systems, quality, support, telemetry, training.
  • Amazon: 24/7 flow, daily management, Lean/Gemba, standard work, approximately five million annual second-day orders.
  • Compunetics: manufacturing, quality, high-reliability delivery, root cause, controls, technical stakeholders.
  • Cardinal: distributed commercial, CRM, fulfillment, logistics, and digital buying workflows.
  • DudeWorth: readiness, decision evidence, governance, and human authority before automation.

AI posture

Use AI to detect patterns, summarize exceptions, surface decision context, support documentation, and improve training only where data, authority, evaluation, and human review are explicit. Do not use AI language as a substitute for process clarity or economics.

First pilot criteria

  • Consequential but bounded.
  • Cross-functional enough to test the operating model.
  • Measurable baseline and credible outcome.
  • Named business owner and decision sponsor.
  • Manageable change burden.
  • Reusable learning even if the intervention stops.

Executive questions

  1. Where does O2C performance currently break the commercial promise most often?
  2. What must the readiness gate be empowered to stop, delay, or condition?
  3. Which KPI definitions are trusted today—and which generate debate instead of action?
  4. Where do urgent exceptions become permanent operating practice?
  5. Which active transformation dependencies could make OPE successful or irrelevant?
  6. What would you need to see after twelve months to believe the function has changed business performance rather than produced activity?

Interview close

“My goal would be to make the transaction easier to understand, safer to change, and harder to break. I would begin by learning where NWN’s commercial promise loses fidelity today, then prove one operating mechanism before asking the organization to adopt a broader model.”
