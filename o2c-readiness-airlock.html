Use this worksheet before a new offering, pricing model, bundle, or go-to-market change enters the transaction engine. For each gate, identify the accountable owner, decision evidence, unresolved dependencies, exceptions, and release condition. The framework is an outside-in candidate hypothesis—not an NWN policy.

1 · Commercial definition

Customer promise, scope, terms, eligibility, dependencies, acceptance, change boundaries.

2 · Margin logic

Price, discounts, cost drivers, cost-to-serve, partner economics, leakage exposure.

3 · Transaction path

Quote, order, fulfillment, acceptance, billing triggers, disputes, exception path.

4 · Finance treatment

Billing model, revenue treatment, evidence, approvals, controls, reconciliation.

5 · System configuration

Salesforce, NetSuite, ServiceNow, integrations, master data, reporting, testing.

6 · Operating adoption

Ownership, SOPs, training, support model, escalation, proficiency, reinforcement.

Release decision

□ Ready to transact

Required evidence and ownership are complete; no unresolved condition materially threatens customer promise, control, or economics.

□ Ready with controls

Known conditions are bounded, time-limited, owned, monitored, and approved with an explicit closure plan.

□ Hold for rework

One or more gaps create unacceptable transaction, finance, customer, system, margin, or adoption risk.

Decision rationale

What evidence changed the decision? What remains open? Who owns closure and by when?
